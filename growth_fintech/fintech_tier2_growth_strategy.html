01 · Market Sizing

The numbers behind Bharat

India's fintech story is usually told through Mumbai, Bangalore, and Delhi. But 57% of India's GDP now comes from Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities, and financial inclusion in these cities is still structurally underserved. The TAM is large and largely unclaimed by product-first fintech companies.

"The Tier-2 fintech user isn't financially unsophisticated. They're financially underserved. They manage money carefully, often in cash, because the digital products built for them were designed for someone else."

02 · User Personas

Two users, two different
jobs to be done

Tier-2 India is not one market. The needs of a 26-year-old salaried employee in Indore are quite different from a 38-year-old local grocery store owner in Coimbatore. Most fintech products pick one and underserve the other.

03 · Funnel Analysis

Where Tier-2 users drop, and why

A standard fintech funnel loses 80-85% of Tier-2 users before their first transaction. The drop-off pattern is different from metro users and needs different fixes.

Priority fixes by stage

Install drop
Reduce APK size for low-storage devices. Add Lite mode. Optimise for 4G with offline-capable flows.
KYC drop
Video KYC in Hindi, Tamil, Telugu. Step-by-step document guide with photos. WhatsApp-assisted KYC as fallback.
Activation drop
Show a personalised credit limit or savings plan immediately after KYC, before asking for any action.
First transaction
Rs 50-100 cashback on first transaction. WhatsApp nudge 24h after KYC if no transaction. Referral bonus for both parties.
Habit drop (D30)
Personalised weekly summary ("You saved Rs 340 this week"). Streak reward for 5 consecutive transactions.
04 · Growth Levers

Six levers, ranked by
impact-to-effort

Tier-2 growth doesn't run on the same playbook as metro fintech. Paid digital CAC doesn't work at the same rates. Trust takes longer to earn. Word-of-mouth and assisted models are where the real leverage is.

01
Referral loops, WhatsApp native
Peer referral is the dominant acquisition channel in Tier-2. Design a WhatsApp-first referral flow: one tap sends a pre-written Hindi or regional language message with a personalised link. Both referrer and referee get Rs 75 on first transaction. The message reads like it's from a friend, not an ad.
Impact: Very HighEffort: LowShips: 3 weeks
P0
02
Vernacular-first onboarding
Full Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, and Kannada support across all critical flows: onboarding, KYC, support, and product explanation. Not just translated UI but culturally adapted copy. "EMI" becomes "kistein," "credit score" becomes "financial trust score." Voice input for forms where typing is a barrier.
Impact: HighEffort: MediumShips: 6 weeks
P0
03
Agent and assisted onboarding model
Partner with local grocery stores, mobile repair shops, and Jan Seva Kendras to become assisted onboarding points. A trained local agent walks users through KYC in person. Agent earns Rs 100 per completed onboarding. Some of the strongest Tier-2 growth stories in Indian fintech came from this kind of assisted model, built on local trust rather than digital ad spend.
Impact: Very HighEffort: HighShips: 10 weeks
P0
04
UPI transaction history as credit signal
Most Tier-2 users are credit-invisible. No CIBIL score, no formal credit history. But they have 12-18 months of UPI transaction data. Build an alternative credit model using transaction frequency, regularity, and merchant diversity. Offer a small starter credit of Rs 2,000-5,000 after 90 days of active usage. This is the product that earns long-term retention.
Impact: Very HighEffort: Very HighShips: 16 weeks
P1
05
Financial literacy content in local language
Short-form video (60 seconds, in local language) explaining one financial concept per week: how to read a bank statement, what a credit score means, how EMIs work. Distributed through WhatsApp and YouTube Shorts. Builds trust before the product ask and frames the brand as an educator rather than just another app.
Impact: MediumEffort: LowShips: 4 weeks
P1
06
Savings gamification for Rajan persona
A "save Rs 10 a day" micro-savings product with a visual jar that fills up. Milestone rewards at Rs 500, Rs 1,000, Rs 5,000. Shareable achievement cards in WhatsApp-native format. Low friction, daily habit, easy to share with friends.
Impact: MediumEffort: MediumShips: 8 weeks
P2

09 · Risks

What could go wrong

High
KYC drop-off kills the funnel before first transaction
WhatsApp-assisted KYC as a fallback. Agent model as a backstop. Reduce required fields to the bare minimum for first-tier approval.
High
Credit NPA rate exceeds model assumptions in Tier-2
Require 90 days of UPI history before any credit offer. Cap initial credit at Rs 2,000. Tighten the model using the first 6 months of real data before scaling.
Medium
Referral program attracts low-quality users who churn after cashback
Release cashback in two parts: Rs 40 on first transaction, Rs 35 on third. Screens for genuine users and limits gaming.
Medium
Agent network quality is inconsistent across cities
Agent rating system with quarterly reviews. Remove agents below 3.5/5. Bonus for agents with 80%+ onboarding completion.
Low
Regulatory change to UPI-based credit scoring
Keep a traditional bureau check running as a parallel model. Build consent-based data architecture from day one. Monitor RBI guidelines quarterly.
10 · What I'd Validate First

Assumptions worth testing
before scaling

Agent model CAC assumption

The model assumes Rs 150-200 CAC for agent-assisted onboarding, based on reported costs from assisted onboarding programmes in Indian fintech from 2019-2021. Those numbers may have shifted. I'd run a 4-week agent pilot in one city before committing to the full network build.

Vernacular as a conversion driver

The strategy assumes vernacular UI materially improves KYC completion. General research supports this but it needs an A/B test: English UI vs Hindi UI vs voice-assisted UI on the same funnel. Run a 3-way split in week 1 of the pilot.
