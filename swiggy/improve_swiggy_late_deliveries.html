Section 01

Executive Summary

Late deliveries are Swiggy's most cited complaint in app store reviews and post-delivery surveys. 26% of all orders arrive after their promised ETA, directly driving cancellations, refund requests, and churn among high-value users. Each late delivery costs Swiggy an average of ₹85 in compensation, support costs, and lost repeat business.

This PRD proposes a three-part intervention: smarter dynamic ETA setting, a proactive delay alert system, and an intelligent delivery routing engine. Together, these reduce late deliveries by an estimated 14 percentage points, saving ₹42 Cr annually and improving D30 retention by 6–9 points.

Problem Statement

Swiggy's current delivery experience suffers from inaccurate ETAs, poor real-time communication during delays, and reactive (not proactive) routing decisions. The result: customers feel misled, not just disappointed. A late delivery users were warned about is forgiven — one they weren't warned about causes churn.

Root Cause Analysis (Fishbone)
❌ Late delivery: order arrives after promised ETA

🍽 Restaurant Delay

  • Underprepared at peak hours
  • Accepting too many orders
  • No live capacity signal to Swiggy
  • Manual order acknowledgement lag

🛵 Rider Delay

  • Long pickup wait at restaurant
  • Poor last-mile routing
  • Multiple simultaneous deliveries
  • Rider unavailability in zone

📡 System/ETA Delay

  • Static ETA ignores live traffic
  • No weather/event adjustment
  • Optimistic ETA to win order
  • No buffer for restaurant prep variance

Initiative 01 — Dynamic ETA Engine (Deep Dive)

How it works: Replace the current static ETA formula with an ML model trained on historical delivery data. Inputs: restaurant avg prep time (rolling 30-day), live Swiggy Maps traffic layer, rider GPS trajectory, weather API, concurrent order count at restaurant, time-of-day/day-of-week patterns.

Key decision: Show a range ETA ("28–35 mins") rather than a point estimate during high-uncertainty orders. This is psychologically proven to reduce dissatisfaction when the upper bound is met.

TensorFlow / XGBoost Real-time traffic API OpenWeather API Rider GPS telemetry Restaurant prep history

Initiative 02 — Proactive Delay Alerts (Deep Dive)

How it works: When the live tracking model detects the order will be ≥5 minutes late, automatically trigger: (1) a push notification with honest ETA update, (2) an in-app delay banner with reason if available, (3) an auto-generated ₹30–₹50 discount coupon for next order if delay ≥15 mins.

Why this matters: Research shows users are 3× more likely to reorder after a late delivery if they received a proactive apology vs. none. The emotional cost of the surprise is higher than the cost of the delay itself.

Section 05

User Stories

As a customer, I want to see a realistic delivery time range before I place my order so that I can decide whether it works for my schedule.
As a customer, I want to receive a push notification immediately if my order will be late, so I'm not left checking the app anxiously.
As a customer, I want to be offered a coupon or credit automatically if my delivery is significantly late, without having to contact support.
As a restaurant partner, I want an easy way to signal that I'm at full capacity so Swiggy stops routing new orders when I'm overwhelmed.
As a delivery rider, I want the app to not assign me a second delivery if my first one will make both orders late.
As a Swiggy ops manager, I want a real-time dashboard showing late-delivery rate by zone, restaurant, and rider so I can intervene quickly.

Section 08

Delivery Roadmap

Week 1–2 · Discovery
Data audit + stakeholder alignment
Analyse 6-month delivery data to quantify root causes. Align with Ops, Restaurants, Rider team, and Data Science on problem framing.
Week 3–6 · Build Phase 1
Proactive Delay Alert system
Quick win. Build delay detection logic, push notification template, and auto-coupon trigger. A/B test alert vs no-alert on 10% of delayed orders.
Week 7–14 · Build Phase 2
Dynamic ETA Engine (ML model)
Data Science builds and validates model. Shadow mode for 2 weeks (compare ML ETA vs old ETA without showing to users). Gradual rollout: 5% → 25% → 100%.
Week 15–20 · Build Phase 3
Smart Rider Batching + Restaurant Capacity API
Rider ops integration, batching model tuning. Restaurant partner app update for capacity signal. Pilot with top 50 restaurant partners in Bangalore.
Week 21–24 · Scale
Full rollout + learnings
Full national rollout. Review metrics vs targets. Publish learnings. Plan Phase 2 improvements (predictive restaurant overbooking prevention).

Section 10

Risks & Mitigations

High
ETA model underperforms in edge cases (festivals, rain, new areas)
Mitigation: Fallback to conservative range ETA when model confidence is low. Add weather and event calendar as explicit features.
High
Restaurant partners resist capacity signal feature
Mitigation: Pilot with willing top partners. Show data that fewer late orders = better ratings = more orders for them.
Med
Delay alert coupon increases cost without proportional retention gain
Mitigation: A/B test coupon value (₹30 vs ₹50) and measure redemption + reorder rate. Cap monthly compensation per user.
Low
Rider earnings drop due to stricter batching limits
Mitigation: Model batching to maintain same average earnings. Monitor weekly; adjust thresholds if earnings dip >3%.
